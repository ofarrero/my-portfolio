Projects

Autonomous Vehicle Project

This project involved the building and programming of an autonomous vehicle. As a group we designed a H-Bridge control board to give power to the motors. Other members of my team soldered this board. My role during the project was the design and implementation of the vehicle controls using an Arduino board. The vehicle followed a line track, to do this I used input from infrared sensors on the front of the vehicle to ensure the wheels turned to stay on the track. I added the capability to break in front of objects to the vehicle using continuos input from a ultrasonic sensor on the front of the vehicle. I also ensured that the vehicle could be controled wirelessly using a XBees,this allowed us to start, stop and get real time information from the vehicle.

Emergency Shelter

In this project we were tasked at making an emergency shelter. The emergency shelter had to be usable by people over the age of 60 with various disabilities including poor eye sight and bad dexterity. Another stipulation of the project was that it had to be made out of mostly recycled materials. The aim of this project was to teach us about the design process and learning how to design inclusively. I kept a design journal for this project, it contained every design we had considered, discussions about materials and manufacturing techniques. During this project we also created several small prototypes so that we could see if our ideas worked and what techniques were best. In the end we had designed and built a foldable pop up shelter with easy access that had a water collection system that could hold up to 2L of water and a frame that could withstand over 1kg being applied to it.

Hands Free Water Filtration System

This project was conducted for Engineers without Boarders. We were tasked with coming up with a solution for a water issue in Zambia. As a team we decided that our project would focus on water filtration in the slums of Lusaka. Lusaka is the capital of Zambia and within the slums nearly all avaiable water has been contaminated due to the use of pit latrines. To counteract this we designed a communal water filtration system. The system used centripital force to force larger debris and dirt to the outside of the device and then used a microfilter to filter out harmful microoganisms. This method allowed the water to be filtered quicker as it was not getting blocked by debris and allowed the filter to last longer for the same reasons. Conceptually this device could produce 220L of water a day and last up to 5 years without any parts having to be replaced.
